What is ATLAS?

ATLAS is an Adaptive Teaching and Learning Assistant System that brings personalized, course-aware AI tutoring to students. Unlike general-purpose AI assistants that rely on broad internet knowledge, ATLAS is deeply rooted in the actual lecture content from your course. The system learns how your instructor explains concepts, the order in which topics are introduced, and the precise terminology used in your course materials.

ATLAS auto-generates a concept dependency map from your course syllabus and lecture transcripts, understanding what must be learned before tackling advanced topics. This means ATLAS knows not just what a concept is, but how it fits into your course and what prerequisites matter for your specific learning journey.

Key Features

Grounded in Lecture Content

Every response is informed by your instructor's actual lectures, not Wikipedia or general internet knowledge.

Concept Dependency Tracking

Auto-generated maps show what you need to learn first and track your progress through course concepts.

Socratic Teaching

ATLAS asks leading questions and guides discovery rather than simply providing answers.

Course Timeline Awareness

The system knows what's been covered in lectures and what's coming up next.

Learning Progress Tracking

Dashboard shows individual and course-wide learning engagement and concept mastery.

Multi-Course Architecture

Manage multiple courses with isolated knowledge bases and student cohorts.

Research & Impact

Active Research Project

ATLAS is an ongoing research initiative at Northeastern University's Arlington campus investigating the effectiveness of course-grounded AI tutoring compared to general-purpose AI assistants. We're measuring learning outcomes, student engagement, concept comprehension, and satisfaction. Our findings contribute to the growing body of evidence about personalized, contextual AI in educational settings.

Principal Investigator: Prof. Bill Claster, Northeastern University
